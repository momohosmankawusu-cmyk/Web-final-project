Crop Guidance

Seasonal Crop Advisories

Expert guidance tailored to Sierra Leone's agricultural seasons — from planting to harvest.

🌾
Rice — Current Season

Preparing Your Paddy Fields

With early rains arriving in the Northern Province, begin field preparation now. Apply organic compost before first ploughing. Watch for blast fungus in low-lying areas.

🥜
Groundnut — Planting Alert

Optimal Planting Window Open

Soil moisture levels in the Eastern Province are ideal for groundnut planting this week. Space rows at 45cm for maximum yield. Avoid waterlogged plots near river banks.

🍠
Cassava — Pest Watch

Mealybug Alert in Southern Districts

Reports of cassava mealybug in Pujehun and Bonthe districts. Apply neem-based spray early morning. Contact your district extension officer for free pesticide allocation.

🌽
Maize — Fertiliser Timing

Top-Dress Fertiliser This Week

Maize crops at 6-leaf stage should receive NPK top-dressing now for optimal cob development. Rain forecast for Thursday in Bo district makes this an ideal application window.

🫚
Palm Oil — Harvest Tip

Identify Ready Bunches

Fresh fruit bunches are ready when 5–10 loose fruits fall to the base. Harvest within 10 days of ripening to maintain oil quality. Store in shaded areas before processing.

🍌
Plantain — Water Management

Mulching to Retain Soil Moisture

During dry spells, apply 10cm thick organic mulch around plantain bases. This reduces water loss by up to 60% and suppresses weed growth without chemical herbicides.

Best Practices

Farming Tips for Sierra Leone

Practical advice to increase yields and protect your land across all seasons.

🌱

Soil Testing

Test your soil pH every 2 seasons. Most Sierra Leone soils are acidic — lime application can increase rice yields by up to 30%.

💧

Water Conservation

Use contour bunding on sloped farms in the Northern Province to reduce runoff by 40% and retain rainwater during dry spells.

🌿

Intercropping

Plant legumes between cassava rows. They fix nitrogen naturally, reducing your fertiliser costs while increasing total farm output.

🐛

Integrated Pest Management

Monitor your crops every 3 days during the growing season. Early pest detection reduces crop loss by 60% before serious damage occurs.

🌾

Post-Harvest Storage

Use hermetic storage bags to protect grain from weevils without chemicals. Reduces post-harvest losses from 30% to under 5%.

📱

Digital Record Keeping

Track your planting dates, inputs, and yields each season. Farmers with records earn 20% more when negotiating with buyers.
